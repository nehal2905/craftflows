What this diagnostic exposes

01 / Framework

Manual work rarely fails loudly. It compounds in 5-minute tasks repeated 40 times a week, in handoffs nobody documented, and in processes that stop when one person is away.

Operational risk scan

03 / Risk

These questions expose concentration risk: workflows that work until they don’t. Answer honestly. The uncomfortable ones are usually the most valuable.

Concentration Risk If your operations manager disappeared tomorrow, what breaks first? Write it here:

Which task can only one employee perform?

How many client-facing emails are sent manually per week?

How often do clients or staff ask “what’s the status?”

How many approvals happen inside WhatsApp or personal texts?

Which process always gets delayed on Mondays?

Which workflow depends entirely on “I’ll do it later”?

Crafted Insight 73% of operational delays we audit trace back to a missing handoff, not a missing tool. The fix is usually routing and triggers, not new software.

Before you build

RULE 01

Fix, then automate

Automation amplifies what exists. Standardize the process manually for two weeks first.

RULE 02

Measure the baseline

Track one week of the manual version. ROI must be a number, not a feeling.

RULE 03

Review quarterly

Tools change. Teams change. 30 minutes per quarter keeps automations an asset.
